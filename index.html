India intelligence
Not a generic tool.
Built for Indian substations.

Most earthing tools are built for Western conditions — homogeneous soil, stable climate, no theft risk. Indian substations are different. Earthing Expert knows the difference.

India-specific knowledge built in
Black cotton soil — high corrosion, seasonal resistivity swings, deep rod penetration challenges. Flags this automatically for Vidarbha, Marathwada, and central India sites.
Coastal and saline soil — chloride-driven galvanic corrosion of GI conductors. Recommends copper and alerts to accelerated degradation risk.
Rocky and laterite terrain — high surface resistivity governs step voltage. Recommends counterpoise and surface layer treatment.
GI conductor theft risk — flags when copper justifies its premium, particularly at critical HV equipment connection points.
EHCC earthing clarity — acceptable as a supplement, never a replacement for a designed grid. This distinction is made every time, unprompted.
CEIG inspection readiness — reminds you of required documentation (IS 3043 compliance, measured earth resistance records, as-built drawings) whenever commissioning is discussed.
Safety philosophy
It will tell you when
something is wrong.

Most tools answer what you ask. Earthing Expert also tells you what you didn't ask — because in EHV earthing, the oversight you didn't notice is the one that causes the accident.

⚠ Safety Hold protocol
When any analysis reveals a condition where touch voltage may be unsafe, bonding is missing, or a system cannot be confirmed safe — it issues a Safety Hold before proceeding. It names the specific condition, specifies the corrective action, and advises involving a licensed EHV engineer for site verification. It does not soften the language. It does not continue the design as if the problem doesn't exist.

Reference: IS 3043 earthing system requirements [Standard Practice]
Verdict: CONDITIONALLY COMPLIANT
Condition: Acceptable as supplement only. A designed grid per

Limitations — be honest
What it cannot do.

Earthing Expert is a preliminary engineering aid. It is not a licensed engineer, a statutory body, or a replacement for site verification.

→ It cannot certify, stamp, or approve a design for statutory or construction purposes. Final sign-off requires a competent licensed engineer.
→ It cannot substitute for site soil testing, physical continuity checks, or field measurements. It uses your data — garbage in, garbage out.
→ Clause citations are provided at confidence level. Where a specific clause cannot be confirmed, it says so and gives a qualitative reference instead.
→ It does not perform finite element analysis or advanced two-layer soil modelling. For complex multi-layer soil or large grid arrays, dedicated software (ETAP, CDEGS) is required.
